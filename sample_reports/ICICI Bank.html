Executive Summary

As of July 11, 2024, ICICI Bank stands as one of India's leading private sector banks, with a comprehensive business model built on multiple revenue streams and a strong digital presence. The bank generates income primarily through three channels: interest income from retail and corporate loans, fee-based services including wealth management and investment banking, and treasury operations involving government securities and foreign exchange trading.

ICICI Bank serves a diverse customer base ranging from individual retail customers and high-net-worth individuals to SMEs, large corporations, NRIs, and rural customers. Its product portfolio is equally extensive, encompassing traditional retail banking services, corporate banking solutions, advanced digital banking platforms, and specialized financial services through its subsidiaries.

What distinguishes ICICI Bank in India's competitive banking landscape is its commitment to technological innovation. As an early adopter of digital banking technologies, the bank has made significant investments in AI, machine learning, and data analytics. Its mobile-first strategy and leadership in digital customer onboarding have established it as a technological pioneer in the sector.
